AI-Assisted Museum Exhibition Classification: Solving the Reflection Problem

üìñ Background

In many museums, exhibits are often encased in glass display cases, causing reflections that blur images and negatively affect automated image recognition accuracy. This presents challenges for real-time identification and classification, particularly in large museums where manually labeling and managing a large volume of artifacts is both time-consuming and labor-intensive.

To address this issue, we have combined the YOLO (You Only Look Once) object detection model with a reflection removal model to create a solution that can adapt to various museum environments, remove reflection interference, and accurately identify artifacts.

‚ú® Our Solution

1. Reflection Removal Model

Background

The primary challenge is dealing with reflection. Traditional open-source reflection removal methods are not effective for handling the complex lighting conditions found in museum exhibition halls. Hence, we developed a model that can adapt to non-fixed environments to effectively remove reflections.

Technique

We adopted the Pix2pix GAN (Generative Adversarial Network) as our foundational architecture and incorporated Sobel Features to enhance the model‚Äôs ability to remove reflections. The model‚Äôs goal is to convert images with reflections into clear, reflection-free photos. This specific design allows the model to focus on reflection areas, avoiding the destruction of essential artifact details.

2. Artifact Identification Model

Background

Even after removing reflections, identifying multiple artifacts in a single image remains challenging. To tackle this, we introduced the YOLOv8n object detection model, which specializes in quick and accurate multi-object detection.

Technique

We manually annotated around 2,000 artifact photos, categorizing them into 7 distinct types of exhibits. Using data augmentation techniques (e.g., flipping, scaling, brightness adjustment), we enhanced the model‚Äôs generalization ability. This treatment enables the model to effectively detect and classify artifacts under various lighting conditions and angles.

3. Combining Reflection Removal and YOLO Detection

The reflection removal model and the YOLOv8n model were combined to provide two forms of artifact identification:

  1. Original Identification: Directly applying YOLO detection on the original photo.
  2. Reflection-Removed Identification: Processing the photo through the reflection removal model first, followed by YOLO detection.

By comparing both results, we select the optimal classification to ensure the best performance in various environments.

üèÜ Results & Achievements

Data Outcomes

  1. V1 Model (Original YOLO): Achieved a classification accuracy of 89% (514/573).
  2. V2 Model (With Reflection Removal): Improved the accuracy to 94.5%, a 1.8% increase over the original model.

Qualitative Outcomes

  1. Focused Attention on Reflections: The reflection removal model accurately identifies and processes reflection areas, improving clarity without damaging artifact details.
  2. Detection Model Performance: The artifact detection model maintains a high level of accuracy in framing and classifying artifacts, even in scenarios with multiple objects.

üì≤ App Design

The reflection removal and YOLOv8n models were deployed in a dual-platform application built with Android Studio and Xcode, allowing users to easily capture images of artifacts and receive instant, detailed information.

Core Features

  1. Camera Functionality: Users can easily access the camera to capture images of exhibits.
  2. Image Processing: After capturing an image, the app simultaneously performs reflection removal and classification, displaying the best results.
  3. Results Display: The app displays the classification and additional information for the detected artifacts.
